Joe Rogan and COVID-19 Deaths in Young, Healthy Individuals

2022-01-17

Intro

With doctors calling on Spotify to stop Joe Rogan Experience misinformation, I wanted to see what his remarks were and what the risk really was.

Summary

Rogan said he did not think young and healthy individuals (specifically a 21 year old) needed to get vaccinated. A rough point estimate of total deaths for individuals aged 0-24, without pre-existing conditions, in England and Wales during March to June 2020 is 15. Data is severely lacking.

Joe’s Remarks

If you’re like 21 years old, and you say to me, ‘Should I get vaccinated?’ I’ll go, ‘No.’”

Joe Rogan

I believe they’re safe and I encourage many people to take them. I just don’t think that if you’re a young healthy person that you need it.

Joe Rogan

Target of Inquiry

Joe is clearly focusing on young, healthy people. We will use people under 25 without pre-existing conditions.

This will only focus on deaths within that category and will not focus on deaths outside the category. This will be a question of individual and not community health.

Data Sources

I found that the UK (specifically England and Wales) has better data sources on deaths of people without pre-existing conditions broken down by age than the USA. Also the USA’s CDC recently corrected its mistake of miscounting millions of vaccine doses.

The UK and USA also have a relatively similar death rate per capita from Covid-19 as the USA, but UK has an older median age (40.6 vs 38.5) and a different racial and ethnic makeup than the USA.

For comparison this means 101 of the 37,641 Covid-19 deaths from March to June 2020 occured in people under 44 without pre-existing conditions, this is 0.268% of the deaths.

0-44?

The only category for young people was ages 0-44, while other age groups have 5 year intervals. This is an unacceptably large range. The UK’s median age is 40, so 0-44 is well over half of its population.

Unfortunately this means we need to estimate the number of deaths by comparing the overall death rate by age group.

0-24 had 31 deaths or 14.4% of the total deaths of ages 0-44 total.

Under 14

For all individuals under 14 (with or without pre-existing conditions) there was 3 deaths total from March to June 2020, for approximately 10 million individuals. That is less than 1 per million per year. For comparison the risk of being struck by lightning is roughly 1 in 500,000.

Making assumptions of estimate the under 24 deaths

We will assume that the proportion of deaths, by age, in the population aged 0-44 is similar to the deaths (by age) of the 0-44 population without complications. This is a problematic assumption as the rate of pre-existing complications increases with age and there may be a interaction between age, pre-existing conditions and death. For example younger aged deaths may be driven by pre-existing conditions to a larger, lessor or similar extent. There are too many unknowns here and the data is not given. It is a rough estimate.

Using this assumption for COVID-19 deaths from March to June 2020 for individuals without pre-existing conditions gives 15 deaths for ages 0-24, which is about 0.04% or 1 in every 2500 deaths from COVID-19.

Summary

Looking at England and Wales between March to June 2020, roughly 15 deaths occurred in individuals under 24 without pre-existing conditions and 3 deaths occurred in individuals under 14 with or without pre-existing conditions.

Data is severely lacking in younger age groups and about individuals without pre-existing conditions.
